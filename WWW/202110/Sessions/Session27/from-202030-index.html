SYLLABUS SETUP PIAZZA (Q&A)
Session 27 Preparation — Videos, Reading and Quizzes

The quizzes are for your LEARNING. They are NOT exams.

  • As long as you do the quiz (and take it seriously), you get FULL CREDIT even if you miss some of the problems.
  • Don't be troubled if your answer is marked wrong. Just BRING YOUR QUESTIONS TO CLASS.

Instructions:

BEFORE this session, for each of the following videos or reading:

  • Watch the video (or read the reading), and as you do so,
  • do the associated quiz in Moodle.

Videos and Reading:

  1. [Sections 1 and 2] Do the Peer Evaluation for Sprint 1 (on Moodle).
  2. Sprint 1 Review: Do whatever your instructor asked you to do (if anything) to review Sprint 1.

    For Sections 1 and 2 this means to do the following for each Card in your Sprint 1 List on your team's Trello Board:

    • If the Card's Feature is complete (or otherwise at a stopping point), have a teammate run the program to test your implementation of the Feature. If the teammate deems that the Feature is working correctly, change the Card's status to Verified by a Peer.
    • If the Card's Feature is complete (or otherwise at a stopping point), have a teammate do a brief Code Review of your implementation of that Feature.
    • If the Card's Feature is NOT complete, make a copy of the Card, putting the copy in Sprint 2 (or Sprint 3 if you will not continue work on the feature until then).
    • Once you have done the above for the Sprint 1 Card, add a Blue stripe to the Card's status to indicate that you have completed your Sprint 1 Review with regard to that Card.
  3. Sprint 2 Planning: Do whatever your instructor asked you to do to plan for Sprint 2.

    For Sections 1 and 2 this means that you should:

    • Determine which Features you expect to complete during Sprint 2. (Coordinate with teammates in making these choices.)
    • Make a Card for each such Feature, by dragging (or copying, your choice) the Card from the Product Backlog List to the Sprint 2 List.
    • For whichever Feature you will work on first, use the Checklist on its Card to list the three or so steps by which you will implement that Feature. Each step should be testable.
  4. Continue to work on your project, being sure to record ALL the hours that you work on the project in your hours-X.txt file.

  5. After doing the above, but before doing the in-class project (in PyCharm):

    • Do this this before-the-session quiz.
    • You do not have to print the quiz (unless you want to). Just hand-write your answers on paper (or type your answers into a file if you prefer).

  6. When you are ready to begin the in-class project (in PyCharm):

    • Open PyCharm. Do:

      VCS ~ Update Project

      to obtain the new in-class project if it is not already in your PyCharm.

    • Watch this start-the-session video.

      • When the video reviews the answers to the before-the-session quiz that you did in the previous step:

        • Check your own answers carefully, marking all that are wrong (for your own sake, not for grading purposes).
        • Take a photo of your marked-up answers and submit it in the Moodle DropBox for that purpose.

      • The video may do some “follow-me” coding.

        • If so, do that coding, either as the video does it or by reviewing the video afterwards (your choice).
        • The key is to UNDERSTAND what you are coding, not merely type what the video shows.
      • Ask questions as desired!
